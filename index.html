How I got into programming.



Intro

When I was 14 years old, I got my first laptop. It was something for me to use on homework and various other projects for school so I didn't have to use my family desktop computer, that my parents used more than I did. So I got one for my birthday and I showed it to a friend of mine. He asked if I had ever used the command window before, and proceeded to show me all of the useful and fun things you could do with batch files, that's the moment I was hooked.




How I got into learning other languages


After making several batch files to do various tasks, my longing for wanting to learn more was growing. I was very much into pc gmaing at this time, and always dreamed of making my own games that my friends and I could play. I did some research and found a few tutorials for unity on how to make simple walking simulators in a 3D space. Creating a virtual space that you are able to move around in that came from your own mind is like a form of art. It was fascinating to me.

Eventually I picked up another program called "Construct 2", which is a program designed for making games and applications in an HTML5 enviroment. This was how I expressed myself, as I was never very good at art in general. I could just make a couple of sprites or grab free ones online and make animations within the editor, and within about an hour, I had made a playable game! My dream had almost come true.




How all of this lead me to LC101


In my senior year of highschool, it was time to start looking at colleges. There was a college fair, and I didn't know where to even start looking for a programming or game design class, but Full-Sail was there and it really caught my interest. Long story short, it ended up being far to expensive for me and I pursued going into electrical school to be an electrician.
I was still tinkering with different laguages at home in my free time to keep the interest alive and continued looking for somewhere I could apply my love of coding to the real world, as game design was not a very profitable route for me. I have a friend that is head of IT in a nearby company and he suggested Launch Code to me. I didn't know what languages I should learn that would be a good introduction for working at a company, but this was a sign that it was what I needed to learn. This is the story from just getting a laptop at 14, until the time and reason you are reading this, as to why I enjoy programming as a hobby.
